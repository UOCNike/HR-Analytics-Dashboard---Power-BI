# HR-Analytics-Dashboard---Power-BI
An interactive HR Analytics dashboard built using Power BI to analyze attrition, employee demographics, and job satisfaction.

---

## Project Overview
This project is an interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard** developed using **Power BI** to analyze workforce data and derive actionable HR insights. The dashboard helps identify employee attrition trends, job satisfaction levels, and demographic distributions across departments and education levels.

## Key Objectives
- Analyze **employee attrition** across departments and age groups
- Understand **job satisfaction ratings** by job role
- Explore **demographic trends** such as age, gender, and education field
- Enable **interactive filtering** using education level slicers

## Tools & Technologies
- **Power BI**
- **Power Query** (Data Cleaning & Transformation)
- **DAX** (Calculated Measures)
- **Microsoft Excel** (Data Source)

## Dataset
- Approximately **1,500 records**
- **41 columns** (40–50% used in final dashboard)
- Data includes employee demographics, job roles, education fields, and attrition status

## Dashboard Components
- KPI Cards (Overall Employees, Attrition, Attrition Rate, Active Employees, Average Age)
- Department-wise Attrition (Pie Chart)
- Employees by Age Group & Gender (Stacked Bar Chart)
- Job Satisfaction Ratings by Job Role (Matrix Heatmap)
- Attrition by Education Field (Bar Chart)
- Attrition Rate by Gender & Age Band (Donut Charts)
- Education Level Slicer for interactivity

## Key DAX Measures
- **Attrition Count**
- **Attrition Rate**
- **Active Employees**

## Key Insights
- Highest attrition observed in **R&D and Sales**
- Employees aged **25–34** show the highest attrition rate
- Job satisfaction varies significantly across job roles
- Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds have higher attrition counts
